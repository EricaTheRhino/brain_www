Facts about Rhinoceroses

Erica is a White Rhinoceros. Rhinos are hunted in the wild by poachers. As a result, the Northern White Rhino is critically endangered, with less than 10 left in the world!

Rhinos are vegetarians. They normally eat up to 50kg of food per day! The black rhino browses on leafy plants, whereas the white rhino lives on grass.

Many rhinos die each year due to illegal poaching. Rhino horn can fetch up to $50,000 on the black market, making it a very lucrative industry. Last year nearly 700 rhinos were killed due to poaching.

The main difference between black and white rhinos is the shape of their mouths - white rhinos have broad flat lips for grazing, whereas black rhinos have long pointed lips for eating foliage.

In the wild, adult rhinoceros have no real natural predators other than humans. Although rhinos are large and have a reputation for being tough, they are very easily poached.

Rhinos are now only found in a few small pockets across Africa, but historically they were spread over a large area. In southern Africa we can see a large area where it is believed both black and white rhinos were in abundance.
